Everthing Is Too Easy After Maxing My Talents
Chapter 189: Ancient Warrior, Resistance Camp!_1

The smoke billowed in the desert, and the sun hung round over the long river. A military pickup truck rumbled along a desolate and deteriorating intercity road, alone on its journey.

This road had been constructed during the government's rule over 300 years ago, once a wide and unobstructed main artery of the city. However, now, it was a narrow, one-way path, eroded by both the relentless passage of time and the encroaching sands of the desert.

This was Wenglado, the desert city of the Red Leaf Empire. According to historical records, more than three centuries ago, it stood as one of the most vital industrial cities in the north of the Red Leaf Empire.

However, during a power struggle, it had become the primary battleground between government forces and powerful corporations. The conflict had reached its zenith, with both sides detonating numerous large nuclear bombs in this region.

Now, all that remained were a few withered plants on the roadside and occasional glimpses of abandoned skyscrapers buried in the desert's embrace.

The wheels of the truck spun through the air, raising yellow sand and intensifying the sense of desolation.

Inside the vehicle, two resistance soldiers dressed as mercenaries glanced at the truck's rearview mirror to ensure there were no pursuers or other vehicles behind them.

The driver, with a hint of relief, said, "We're safe here. You can come out and get some fresh air."

Upon hearing this, Sun Ruo unwrapped the special nano-material covering around her and, with a red face, stuck her head out for a breath of fresh air. "It's like three layers inside and three layers outside; I was almost suffocating!"

The resistance soldier driving chuckled and commented, "This precaution is for your safety. Without disguising you so thoroughly, it would be impossible to evade the security of the Yunxiao Corporation."

In the container behind the truck, Li Yueming also poked his head out. When Sun Ruo met his gaze, she couldn't help but recall their intimate contact just moments ago, and her face reddened. After a brief moment of awkwardness with Li Yueming, she broke the silence, asking impatiently, "What special talent have you awakened? Let's see if it's among the first-rank talents."

Li Yueming glanced at her and replied casually, "Martial Saint of the Wilderness. Do you understand?"

Upon hearing this, Sun Ruo couldn't hide her confusion and asked, "You've never heard of it, have you? Well, given your limited horizons, that's not surprising."

Sun Ruo felt slighted, and to downplay her confusion, she added, "I haven't heard of a talent like Martial Saint of the Wilderness, but I've heard of ancient martial art practitioners. Maybe your talent is related to them."

Li Yueming's expression shifted when he heard the phrase "ancient martial arts practitioners." He asked with keen interest, "Ancient martial arts practitioners? Tell me more."

Sun Ruo turned red again and stammered, "I heard my father mention it when I was young. I don't know the details."

Li Yueming rolled his eyes, signaling that it was a sensitive topic, considering Sun Ruo's parents had passed away. He chose to disregard her and turned his attention to the desert scenery outside the pickup.

In the front seats, the two rebel mercenaries, Takagi and Takata, were responsible for transporting supplies between East Peak Mountain and N City, all while maintaining their secret identities. They discussed the journey ahead, explaining that they had taken a remote, uncontrolled road to avoid detection. If all went well, they expected to reach their destination in ten days. However, they might encounter trouble, such as being targeted by scavengers in nearby rogue gathering areas.

As the truck continued on its path through the desolation, the trio contemplated the challenges that lay ahead in their escape from the grasp of the Yunxiao Corporation.
